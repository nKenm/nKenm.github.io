Over-Simplified Version to build a PC

  1. Bring out the Motherboard and CPU of choice and place it in the socket.
  2. Before putting the motherboard in the case take this time to do some cable management in the case, place in the case Fans, power supply cables, if you have a radiator also begin positioning it AND if your motherboard includes a IO Shield make sure to place it before putting in the motherboard in the case.
  3. Once the cable management is done place in your motherboard to its respective place WITH motherboard standoffs attached, if you do not do this you may short circuit your board and ruin it.
  4. After it begin placing the RAM slots in their position, they should not be next to each other but space out from one another.
  5. Once done make sure you put in the CPU Cooler with either the thermal paste that is pre-applied to it or.
  6. After that put in the graphics card into the first PCIE x 16 slot to use the full bandwidth.
  7. Look for the HDD/SSD location on your Case and plug in the power and SATA cables from the drive to motherboard, depending how many you bought.
  8. Finally plug in the 24+2 power pin connector to the motherboard, usually located at the right-upper side of the board and the CPU power in the Left-upper side. And plug in the power cord into the GPU, unless it does require one.
  9. Now you may turn on your PC and hope for the BIOS logo, if it does you have successfully build a computer, if not then it'll be troubleshooting time.
